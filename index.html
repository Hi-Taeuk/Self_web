※ Incheon, the international city of Northeast Asia

A metropolitan city in South Korea. It is the outer port of Seoul, the capital city.
It is located in northwestern South Korea and the central western part of the Korean Peninsula.
It is the second largest port city after Busan, the largest coastal city in the metropolitan area,
and the third local government. It is the third most populous metropolitan government after Seoul and Busan.
With Incheon International Airport and Incheon Port International Passenger Terminal,
it is the first stop for most foreign travelers visiting Korea.
Incheon International Airport is connected to countries on the world's major continents,
including Asia, America, Europe, Oceania, and Africa, and Incheon Port is mainly connected to China.
The eponymous tree is a tree lily, the eponymous flower is a rose, and the ancestral ancestor is a crane.
The crane is known as a bird with a deep connection to Incheon, with many regions in the Incheon region having the name 'crane',
and it also served as the motif for UT, the mascot of Incheon United in the K League. However,
due to the destruction of the ecosystem caused by urban development in Incheon, cranes disappeared from mainland Incheon,
and about 10 cranes arrive every year in Ganghwa-gun, far away from the city.

※ Incheon International Airport

Incheon International Airport is Korea's central airport,
located at 272 Gonghang-ro and 446 Airport Terminal-daero, Jung-gu, Incheon,
in accordance with the Seoul Metropolitan Area New Airport Construction Promotion Act.
It opened on March 29, 2001, taking over the international function of Gimpo International Airport.
It is the largest airport in Korea and is considered the gateway to Korea
in that most major international flights are connected through this airport. In addition,
it is a world-class airport that has consistently ranked at the top in various indicators for airports around the world
since its opening.

※ Chinatown in Incheon!

It is loved as a landmark tourist destination that represents Incheon Metropolitan City and Jung-gu in name and reality. When you say 'Chinatown' in Korea, many people choose this place. There are Chinatowns in Seoul, Busan, and Daegu, but Incheon's Chinatown is overwhelmingly large and is known as Korea's representative Chinatown because it is the birthplace of Jjajangmyeon, a representative Chinese food in Korea.

※ Most shops on an underground floor! Bupyeong

It is an underground shopping mall with a world record.
To be precise, it held the record for ‘the largest number of stores opened in an underground space within
a single space.’ In May 2014, it was recognized as the single building with the most stores
in Korea by the Korea Record Institute, and in October 2014, it was recognized as a world record.
The number of stores was 1,408 as of May 2, 2014. If you actually go there, you can see that
there are a lot of stores attached to each other and you can feel that there are quite a lot of them.
